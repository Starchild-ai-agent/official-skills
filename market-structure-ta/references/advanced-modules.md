# Advanced SMC / ICT Modules

Use these when a basic OB/FVG/sweep setup needs upgrading to A+ quality.

## 1. Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)
Fibonacci-based pullback zone for trend continuation entries.
- **Zone:** 0.62 – 0.79 retracement of the most recent impulse leg
- **Sweet spot:** 0.705 (the "ICT golden ratio")
- **How to use:** After a confirmed BOS, wait for pullback into OTE; combine with FVG/OB inside the zone for highest probability
- **Stop:** beyond the 0.79 level (or beyond the structure that created the leg)
- **Target:** prior swing high/low (for 1.0 extension) or 1.618/2.0 extension

## 2. Power of Three (PO3) / AMD
Every meaningful price move follows three phases:
1. **Accumulation** — sideways consolidation, range builds, low volatility (institutions building positions)
2. **Manipulation** — false breakout in the WRONG direction, sweeping liquidity (the "Judas swing")
3. **Distribution / Expansion** — true move in the intended direction with momentum

Most retail traders enter during manipulation thinking it's the real move. Smart money enters on the reversal after the sweep. Look for PO3 on daily candles first, then session candles (Asia / London / NY).

## 3. Unicorn Model
The A+ confluence setup. Requires:
- A **Breaker Block** (failed order block that price retraded)
- Overlapping with a **Fair Value Gap** inside it
- Located at a key HTF level
- Untested since formation

When all four align, it's a Unicorn. High win-rate trend continuation entry.

## 4. Breaker Block
An order block that **failed to hold** — price broke through it, then comes back.
- Original bullish OB at $X. Price drops below $X (OB fails), then rallies back.
- The OB now flips: it acts as **resistance on the way back up** = bearish breaker
- Trade direction: opposite of the original OB
- Why it works: trapped longs from the original OB are looking to exit at breakeven (selling pressure)

## 5. Mitigation Block
Similar idea but applied to swing highs/lows where positions were trapped.
- A swing high formed at $X with longs trapped
- Price drops, then returns to $X area
- Trapped longs exit at breakeven → selling pressure → reversal
- Entry: at the mitigation block edge, stop beyond the trapped area

## 6. Silver Bullet
ICT's high-probability intraday window (US session focus).
- **Time:** 10:00 – 11:00 ET (also 14:00 – 15:00 ET as second window)
- **Setup:** Wait for liquidity sweep + FVG inside the killzone
- **Direction:** trade the FVG fill back to recent swing
- Stop tight (15–30 pip on FX, ~0.3% on equities)

## 7. Judas Swing
Early-session false move designed to sweep stops before the real trend.
- Typically NY open (09:30 ET) or London open (03:00 ET)
- Price spikes one direction, sweeps liquidity, then reverses violently
- Wait for the sweep + CHoCH on 5m/1m, enter the reversal

## 9. Inversion Fair Value Gap (IFVG)
When a bullish FVG gets violated (price closes through it), it flips bearish — and vice versa.
- Now acts as resistance on the way back up
- Useful for trading the second leg after a failed setup

## 10. Liquidity Void
Large gap-like move with little structure inside. Price often returns to fill it before continuing.
- Forms during news events or session opens
- Mark the entire void, expect partial or full fill on the next consolidation

## Setup Quality Tier List

| Tier | Setup | Description |
|---|---|---|
| A+ | Unicorn at HTF POI | Breaker + FVG overlap inside HTF OB, post-sweep |
| A | OB + FVG + Sweep | Standard SMC trifecta in killzone |
| B | OB alone in OTE | Decent if HTF trend aligns |
| B | FVG fill in trend | Trade with the trend, weaker confluence |
| C | OB or FVG counter-trend | Lower probability; scalp only |
| F | "Looks like an OB" without BOS | NOT an order block — skip |

The grade dictates position size, not whether to take it. Take A+ at full size, B at half, C at quarter (if at all).
