# Wyckoff Schematics Reference

The cycle: **Accumulation → Markup → Distribution → Markdown → repeat**.

## Accumulation (Bottom Pattern)

Smart money buys from retail panic sellers. Forms a trading range (TR).

### Phase Events
| Event | Acronym | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Preliminary Support | **PS** | First sign downtrend is slowing; large volume, wide spread |
| Selling Climax | **SC** | Capitulation low; massive volume, very wide spread |
| Automatic Rally | **AR** | Bounce off SC; defines the upper bound of TR |
| Secondary Test | **ST** | Retest of SC area on lower volume = supply exhausted |
| Spring | (optional) | False breakdown below TR low; traps shorts, sweeps SSL |
| Test | — | Low-volume retest of Spring low confirms strength |
| Sign of Strength | **SOS** | Rally on expanding volume, breaks above prior resistance |
| Last Point of Support | **LPS** | Pullback to former resistance (now support) before markup |

### Phases
- **A:** stopping action (PS, SC, AR, ST)
- **B:** building cause (consolidation, multiple tests within TR)
- **C:** test of supply (Spring or Last Point of Supply)
- **D:** markup begins (SOS, LPS)
- **E:** trend out of TR (markup phase proper)

**Where to buy:** at Spring/Test (Phase C, aggressive) or at LPS (Phase D, confirmed).

## Distribution (Top Pattern)

Mirror image. Smart money sells to retail enthusiasm.

### Phase Events
| Event | Acronym | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Preliminary Supply | **PSY** | First sign uptrend is slowing |
| Buying Climax | **BC** | Euphoric high, wide spread, huge volume |
| Automatic Reaction | **AR** | Pullback; defines lower bound of TR |
| Secondary Test | **ST** | Retest of BC on lower volume = demand exhausted |
| Upthrust | **UT** | False breakout above TR high; traps longs |
| Upthrust After Distribution | **UTAD** | Higher-quality version of UT (often final high) |
| Sign of Weakness | **SOW** | Breakdown on expanding volume |
| Last Point of Supply | **LPSY** | Bounce to former support (now resistance) before markdown |

### Phases
- **A:** stopping action
- **B:** building cause (sideways, multiple UTs and STs)
- **C:** test of demand (UTAD)
- **D:** markdown begins (SOW, LPSY)
- **E:** trend down

**Where to short:** at UTAD (Phase C) or LPSY (Phase D).

## Three Wyckoff Laws

1. **Supply & Demand** — price rises when demand > supply; falls when supply > demand. Read VOLUME, not just price.
2. **Cause & Effect** — the size of the trading range (cause) determines the size of the eventual trend (effect). Bigger sideways = bigger trend.
3. **Effort vs Result** — volume (effort) should produce commensurate price movement (result). Divergence = warning sign (e.g. high volume but small range = absorption).

## Volume Reading
- **No demand bar** (bullish): narrow range, low volume in an uptrend pullback = supply exhausted, expect continuation up
- **No supply bar** (bearish equivalent): narrow range, low volume in a downtrend pullback = demand exhausted
- **Climactic volume**: a single bar with volume 3x+ average often marks an exhaustion point
- **Absorption**: high volume but small range = aggressive activity hidden by counter-flow

## Quick Phase ID Checklist
Looking at a chart and need to ID the phase fast?
1. **Trending or ranging?** Trending = markup or markdown. Ranging = accumulation or distribution.
2. **If ranging, what was the prior trend?** Down → likely accumulation. Up → likely distribution.
3. **Within the range, look for the climax (SC or BC)** — usually the bar with highest volume + widest spread.
4. **Spring or UT yet?** No → still in Phase B. Yes → Phase C.
5. **Breakout occurred on volume?** Yes → Phase D/E.

## Combining with SMC
- Wyckoff Spring = SMC liquidity sweep of SSL
- Wyckoff UTAD = SMC liquidity sweep of BSL
- SOS / SOW = SMC Break of Structure (BOS)
- LPS / LPSY = SMC pullback to OB / FVG

The frameworks describe the same thing in different languages. Use Wyckoff for the macro story, SMC for the precise entry levels.
