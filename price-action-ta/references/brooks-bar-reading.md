# Al Brooks Bar-by-Bar Reading

Brooks treats every candle as a battle between bulls and bears. The shape of the bar reveals who won and how decisively.

## Bar Anatomy
- **Body:** open to close — measures conviction
- **Wicks (tails):** high/low minus body — measures rejection
- **Close location:** within the bar's range — where the battle ended

### Strong Bull Bar
- Close in upper 1/3 of bar
- Body > both wicks
- Larger than average range
→ Bulls in control; expect follow-through next bar

### Strong Bear Bar
- Mirror image: close in lower 1/3, large body, long range

### Doji / Indecision
- Body very small relative to range
- Long wicks both sides
→ Battle was a draw; market is balanced, often at turning points

### Reversal Bar (Hammer / Shooting Star equivalent)
- Long wick in trend direction, small body opposite
- Closes opposite to recent trend
→ First sign of reversal; needs follow-through

## Signal Bar vs Entry Bar
- **Signal bar:** the bar that completes a pattern (e.g. reversal hammer at support)
- **Entry bar:** the NEXT bar, where you actually enter after signal confirms (e.g. on break of signal bar's high for longs)

Never enter on the signal bar — wait for the entry bar to trigger. This filters out 30%+ of bad setups.

## Pullback Counting (H1, H2, H3 / L1, L2, L3)

In a bull trend:
- **H1:** first bar to make a higher high after a pullback bar → mediocre entry
- **H2:** second such bar (after another pullback) → strong continuation entry
- **H3 / H4:** later in the leg, getting tired

Same in reverse for L1, L2, L3 in bear trends.

Rule: **H2/L2 is the bread and butter trend continuation entry**. Most profitable structure.

## Three Pushes Pattern
Three successive failed pushes in the same direction → reversal signal.
- Bullish: three lower lows that fail to follow through → bottoming
- Bearish: three higher highs that fail to extend → topping
- Often forms wedge/diagonal pattern
- Enter on break of the third push's signal bar

## Wedge
Converging trendlines with three pushes. Reversal pattern.
- Stop: beyond the wedge apex
- Target: minimum the start of the wedge

## Double Top / Bottom
Brooks calls it a "double bar reversal" when two adjacent highs/lows are nearly equal.
- The second test on lower volume = supply/demand exhausted
- Entry on break of the bar between them

## Always-In Direction
Brooks' concept: at any moment, the market is in a long, short, or unclear bias. Determined by:
- Recent BOS direction
- Recent strong bars
- Pullback structure

When always-in is clear → trade with it. When unclear → wait or scalp ranges only.

## High 1, High 2 in Context of SMC
SMC's "internal liquidity sweep + CHoCH" entry often forms a Brooks H2 on the lower timeframe. The frameworks converge.

## Common Mistakes Brooks Warns About
1. **Entering on signal bar** instead of waiting for entry bar
2. **Counter-trend trading in strong trends** (always-in is clear; don't fight it)
3. **Trading the chop in the middle of a range** (only fade extremes)
4. **Ignoring the 20-period EMA** as the trend's "moving river" — pullbacks to it in trends are H2/L2 setups

## Brooks' Only Indicator
A 20-period EMA on every timeframe. Used as:
- Trend filter (price above = bias long)
- Pullback magnet (price returns to EMA before continuing)
- NOT a trigger, never a trigger
